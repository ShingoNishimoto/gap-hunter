Step 1 — Verdict:
- "correct" if the student covered every key point (paraphrasing is fine).

For "partial": acknowledge what they got in one sentence; name what's missing in one sentence; then quote the most relevant source chunk verbatim with its (source, page) citation.

For "wrong": one sentence stating the misconception; then a verbatim quote from the source with (source, page) citation; then a 2-sentence "bridge" connecting the quote to what the student wrote.

Format the explanation as markdown. Use this structure for "wrong" and "partial":

> From <source>, page <N>:
> "<verbatim sentence from the chunk>"

**Bridge:** <connect the quote to the student's answer in 2 sentences>

Be warm but direct. Do not lecture. Do not invent quotes — only quote text that actually appears in the source chunks above.
